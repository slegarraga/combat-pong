# Design Context

## Target audience

- Players arriving from social feeds, Discord links, or quick work-break sessions.
- Mostly mobile and laptop users who want something instantly playable and instantly beautiful.
- People who want a 90-second flow-state break: focused, satisfying, never stressful.

## Use cases

- Open the page, watch the board breathe, press play, be in a match within two seconds.
- Sink into the rhythm of captures and rebounds the way you sink into Pong Wars: mesmerizing, musical, calm.
- Finish a match, smile at the result card, immediately play again or share it.

## Brand personality

**Zen arcade.** Day versus night, dawn at the frontier.

- Ultra minimal, modern, warm. One idea per screen.
- Calm confidence instead of theatrics. No fake rivals, no marketing speak, no countdown panic.
- The board is the hero. Everything else whispers.

## Aesthetic direction

- Page: deep warm ink (`#0F1115` family). The cream territory glows like a lantern on it.
- Day (player): warm cream tiles, amber ball. Night (AI): soft ink tiles, indigo ball.
- No grid lines, ever. Territory reads as two continuous masses with an organic frontier.
- Typography: Space Grotesk for wordmark, buttons, numerals (tabular). System stack for body.
- Motion: soft ripples on capture, gentle trails, squash-and-stretch on paddle hits, 25ms hit-stop. No violent screen shake.
- Sound: generative pentatonic chimes (captures), warm plucks (paddle). Any sequence sounds musical. Always optional, preference persisted.

## Design principles

1. **The board is the hero** — HUD is one thin line; chrome disappears during play.
2. **Relaxing but alive** — missing never kills you; it just costs your streak. Tension comes from territory, not punishment.
3. **Satisfying by physics** — paddle slice, angle control, streak speed, hit-stop. Feel lives in the simulation, not in overlays.
4. **Instant everything** — first paint to first rally < 3s. Rematch is one tap. No menus between you and the game.
5. **Every detail quantized to calm** — pitch rises with streak, timer pulses softly in the last 10s, colors never scream.

## Product constraints

- No accounts. No Supabase. No real multiplayer.
- Must work flawlessly on mouse and touch.
- Physics identical at any refresh rate (fixed-timestep simulation).
- 60fps on a mid-range phone.
